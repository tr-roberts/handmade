What is a Negroni?

A Negroni is an Italian cocktail, made of one part gin, one part vermouth Rosso (red, semi-sweet), and one part Campari, garnished with orange peel. It is considered an aperitif. A traditionally made Negroni is stirred, not shaken; it is built over ice in an old-fashioned or rocks glass and garnished with a slice of orange. Outside of Italy, orange peel is often used in place of an orange slice.

How to Make a Negroni

Ingredients

  • - Dry Gin (Preferably Monkey 47 Dry Gin)
  • - Italian Red Bitter Spirit (Preferably Campari)
  • - Sweet Vermouth (Preferably Punt e Mes)
  • - Orange Slice
  • - Ice

Instructions

  • - Add ice to an old-fashioned glass
  • - Then, add all liquid ingredients in any order
  • - Then, stir lightly with a spoon
  • - Finally, garnish with a slice of orange
